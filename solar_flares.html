Solar Flares: The Sun's Fury

An interactive guide to their impact on Earth

What is a Solar Flare?

A solar flare is an intense burst of radiation coming from the release of magnetic energy associated with sunspots. They are our solar system's largest explosive events. The light from a flare reaches Earth in just 8 minutes.

However, the most significant impacts come from associated Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs), giant clouds of charged particles that can take 1 to 4 days to reach Earth.

Earth's Protective Shield: The Magnetosphere

Thankfully, Earth has a natural defense system: the magnetosphere. This magnetic field deflects most of the harmful charged particles from the Sun, protecting life on the surface. But powerful CMEs can still disturb this shield and have noticeable effects.
