Cosmic Rays

Exploring the high-energy particles raining down from space

What Are Cosmic Rays?

Cosmic rays are highly energetic atomic nuclei and other particles traveling through space at nearly the speed of light. They are not "rays" but physical particles. They originate from the Sun, from outside of the solar system in our own galaxy (Galactic Cosmic Rays or GCRs), and from distant galaxies.

When these particles slam into Earth's atmosphere, they create a shower of secondary particles that rain down through the air, some of which can be detected at the surface.

Earth's Double Shield

Fortunately, we are well-protected from the constant cosmic ray bombardment. Earth has two main layers of defense:

  • The Magnetosphere: This magnetic field deflects the majority of lower-energy cosmic rays, shielding the planet from a significant amount of radiation.
  • The Atmosphere: The thick blanket of air above us acts as a physical shield. Cosmic rays that make it through the magnetosphere collide with air molecules, losing energy and breaking apart into less harmful secondary particles.
